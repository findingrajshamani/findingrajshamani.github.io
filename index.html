Hi Raj,

I wasn't planning to build a website.

I was trying to answer a question.

Somewhere along the way, answering it turned into this.

If I wanted to introduce myself, maybe the best first message wasn't another message at all.

Maybe it was building something.

So I built this.

But building things has always been my way of creating opportunities instead of waiting for them.

Over the last few years, curiosity has pulled me into digital products, automation, creators, and a few ideas that were probably more ambitious than practical.

One story of yours that stayed with me was when you talked about observing people in supermarkets and noticing buying patterns.

What stuck with me wasn't really the supermarket part. It was the curiosity behind it—the habit of paying attention to things most people walk past.

I related to that. Not because I intentionally spent time studying supermarket shelves, but because I've always found myself wondering how things work, why they work, and how they could work better.

A lot of that curiosity eventually turned into building things. Most of what I built didn't work. That was kind of the point.

Recently, I've become obsessed with a question. That question eventually turned into something I'm building—and funny enough, it led me closer to your world than I expected.

I'll leave it at that.

I could tell you everything I've built. I could tell you every idea I'm working on.

But I'd rather leave that for an actual conversation.
